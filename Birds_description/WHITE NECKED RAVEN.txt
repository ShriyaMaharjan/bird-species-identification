The White-necked Raven (Corvus albicollis) is native to eastern and southern Africa, inhabiting a range that includes countries such as Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, and South Africa. These large and intelligent corvids are adaptable to various environments, from savannas and grasslands to mountainous regions.

Characteristics:
1. **Distinctive Plumage:** White-necked Ravens are predominantly black with a glossy sheen on their feathers. As the name suggests, they have a striking white patch on the back of their neck, which is a distinctive feature that sets them apart from other raven species.

2. **Large Size:** These ravens are among the larger members of the corvid family, with a wingspan reaching over three feet. Their size contributes to their dominance in their habitats, and they are often observed as powerful and agile flyers.

3. **Highly Intelligent:** White-necked Ravens are renowned for their intelligence and problem-solving abilities. They have demonstrated the capacity to use tools and adapt their behaviors to changing environmental conditions, showcasing their cognitive flexibility.

4. **Varied Diet:** White-necked Ravens are omnivores with a diverse diet. They feed on a wide range of food items, including carrion, small mammals, insects, fruits, and grains. Their adaptability to different food sources contributes to their ability to thrive in various ecosystems.

5. **Vocal Mimicry:** Like many corvids, White-necked Ravens are adept mimics. They can imitate a variety of sounds, including other bird species, animals, and even human-made noises. Their vocal repertoire plays a role in communication within their social groups and may serve as a means of attracting mates or signaling danger.
