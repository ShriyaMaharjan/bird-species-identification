The Crested Nuthatch (Sitta frontalis) is a bird species native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in countries such as India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of Southeast Asia. Inhabiting various forested habitats, including deciduous and evergreen forests, this nuthatch is characterized by its unique features and foraging behavior.

1. **Size:** The Crested Nuthatch is a relatively small bird, with adults typically measuring around 13 to 15 centimeters in length.

2. **Plumage:** Identified by its distinct coloration, this nuthatch species has a blue-grey upper body, a black cap on the head, and a distinctive long black stripe running from the eye down the neck, giving it a crested appearance.

3. **Foraging Technique:** Crested Nuthatches are known for their distinctive foraging behavior, which involves moving head-first down tree trunks and branches in search of insects, seeds, and small invertebrates. Their strong bills and sharp claws aid in this unique foraging technique.

4. **Vocalizations:** These nuthatches are vocal birds, producing a variety of calls and songs. Their vocal repertoire includes sharp "tink" sounds and nasal calls, which serve for communication within pairs and during territorial displays.

5. **Habitat:** Crested Nuthatches are adaptable to a range of wooded environments, from primary and secondary forests to gardens and parks. They are often found in areas with suitable trees for foraging and nesting.

These characteristics collectively define the Crested Nuthatch as a visually distinctive and adaptable bird species within the diverse avian communities of the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.
