Origin:
The Gray Kingbird (Tyrannus dominicensis) is a species of bird native to the Americas, primarily found in the Caribbean islands, as well as parts of Central and South America. It inhabits a variety of habitats including coastal areas, forests, and open woodlands, where it perches prominently to hunt for flying insects. Despite its name, the Gray Kingbird is known for its vibrant personality and assertive behavior, often seen vigorously defending its territory from intruders.

Characteristics:
1. Plumage: The Gray Kingbird has predominantly gray plumage on its upperparts, with a white throat and underparts. It features a distinctive black mask around its eyes and a white band on its tail, which is visible in flight.
2. Size: It is a medium-sized bird, measuring around 8 to 9 inches (20 to 23 centimeters) in length, with a wingspan of approximately 12 to 14 inches (30 to 36 centimeters).
3. Feeding Behavior: The Gray Kingbird primarily feeds on flying insects such as bees, wasps, and dragonflies, which it catches in mid-air using agile flight maneuvers. It also consumes berries and fruits, especially during the non-breeding season.
4. Vocalizations: Known for its loud and melodious calls, the Gray Kingbird produces a variety of vocalizations including sharp "ki-dik" or "keer" calls, often repeated in rapid succession.
5. Breeding Habits: Constructs cup-shaped nests made of twigs, grasses, and other plant materials, typically placed in trees or shrubs. Both parents participate in incubating the eggs and caring for the young hatchlings until they fledge.
