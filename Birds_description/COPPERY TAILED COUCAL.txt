The Coppery-tailed Coucal (Centropus cupreicaudus) is a bird species native to sub-Saharan Africa, particularly found in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. Inhabiting dense vegetation, marshes, and wetlands, this coucal species is known for its striking appearance and distinctive behaviors.

1. **Size:** The Coppery-tailed Coucal is a medium-sized bird, with adults typically measuring around 43 to 46 centimeters in length.

2. **Plumage:** Characterized by its unique coloration, this coucal species has a predominantly black body with a coppery-red or chestnut-colored tail. The combination of dark and vibrant hues contributes to its visually striking appearance.

3. **Habitat:** Coppery-tailed Coucals are well-adapted to wetland habitats, including reed beds, swamps, and dense vegetation along water bodies. They are often found near water sources where their foraging and nesting needs are met.

4. **Foraging Behavior:** These coucals are skilled hunters, feeding on a varied diet of insects, small vertebrates, and amphibians. Their foraging behavior involves moving through dense vegetation and utilizing their strong bills to catch prey.

5. **Vocalizations:** Coppery-tailed Coucals are known for their distinctive calls, which include a series of deep and resonant booming sounds. Their vocalizations are integral to communication within pairs and territories, especially during the breeding season.

These characteristics collectively define the Coppery-tailed Coucal as an aesthetically captivating and ecologically adapted bird species within the diverse landscapes of sub-Saharan Africa.
