The Cuban Trogon (Priotelus temnurus) is a bird species endemic to the island of Cuba in the Caribbean. This trogon, often considered the national bird of Cuba, is recognized for its vibrant plumage and can be found in various habitats, including forests and woodlands across the island.

1. **Size:** The Cuban Trogon is a medium-sized bird, typically measuring around 28 to 32 centimeters in length.

2. **Plumage:** Its plumage is characterized by a striking combination of colors. The upperparts are iridescent green, while the underparts feature a bright red belly and breast. The head displays a distinctive white and black pattern.

3. **Habitat:** Cuban Trogons inhabit a range of forested environments, including both montane and lowland forests. They are often associated with areas containing suitable nesting sites, such as tree hollows.

4. **Diet:** These trogons primarily feed on a diet of insects and small fruits. Their foraging behavior involves searching for prey among the foliage and branches of trees.

5. **Nesting Habits:** Cuban Trogons are cavity nesters, utilizing natural hollows in trees or old woodpecker nests for breeding. The female typically lays a small clutch of eggs, and both parents participate in incubation and raising the offspring.

These characteristics collectively contribute to the Cuban Trogon's unique identity as a symbol of avian biodiversity in Cuba, showcasing its vibrant plumage and adaptability to various forested habitats on the island.
