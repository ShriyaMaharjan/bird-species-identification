The Crested Fireback (Lophura ignita) is a pheasant species native to Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. Inhabiting dense tropical forests, this bird is recognized for its vibrant appearance and distinctive features.

1. **Size:** Crested Firebacks are medium-sized birds, with males typically measuring around 70 to 76 centimeters in length, including their long tail feathers.

2. **Plumage:** Characterized by striking coloration, the male Crested Fireback has glossy black plumage with prominent white markings on the wings and back. The crest on its head adds to its regal appearance. Females, in contrast, have more subdued brown plumage.

3. **Habitat:** These firebacks prefer lowland tropical forests with dense undergrowth, where they can forage and seek shelter. They are well-adapted to the diverse environments of their native Southeast Asian habitats.

4. **Diet:** Crested Firebacks are omnivorous, feeding on a diet that includes fruits, seeds, insects, and small invertebrates. Their foraging behavior involves scratching the forest floor in search of food.

5. **Courtship Display:** During the breeding season, males perform elaborate courtship displays, including spreading their vibrant tail feathers and calling to attract females. These displays contribute to the visual spectacle of their mating rituals.

These characteristics collectively define the Crested Fireback as a visually striking and emblematic species within the rich avian diversity of Southeast Asian tropical forests.
