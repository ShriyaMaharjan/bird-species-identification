The Venezuelan Troupial (Icterus icterus) is native to northern South America, particularly Venezuela and parts of Colombia. These striking birds inhabit a variety of environments, including open woodlands, savannas, and urban areas, where they are known for their vibrant plumage and distinctive behaviors.

Characteristics:
1. **Vibrant Plumage:** The Venezuelan Troupial is characterized by its vibrant and contrasting plumage, with bright orange-yellow on the head, neck, and upper breast, contrasting with black wings, tail, and lower body. This coloration contributes to their overall eye-catching appearance.

2. **Large Size:** Troupials are relatively large orioles, with a length ranging from 23 to 32 cm (9 to 12.5 inches). Their robust build and prominent size make them stand out among other bird species in their habitat.

3. **Distinctive Song:** These birds are known for their melodious and varied songs, which may include whistles, chatters, and mimicry of other bird species. Their vocalizations play a role in communication, especially during the breeding season.

4. **Omnivorous Diet:** Venezuelan Troupials have an omnivorous diet, feeding on a variety of foods such as fruits, nectar, insects, and small vertebrates. Their adaptability to different food sources contributes to their success in diverse habitats.

5. **Elaborate Nesting:** Troupials are known for their elaborate hanging nests, which are woven from plant fibers and often placed in palm trees or other tall vegetation. The construction of these nests is a collaborative effort between the male and female, showcasing their intricate nesting behavior.
