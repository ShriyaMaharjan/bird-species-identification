The Banded Broadbill, scientifically known as Eurylaimus javanicus, is a species of bird native to Southeast Asia. These broadbills are primarily found in regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and Myanmar. Banded Broadbills inhabit various forested habitats, including tropical rainforests and montane forests, where they forage for insects and small vertebrates. They are known for their distinctive appearance, characterized by their colorful plumage, broad bills, and prominent black and white bands across their chests.

1. Plumage: The Banded Broadbill displays vibrant plumage, with shades of green, blue, and yellow, complemented by bold black and white bands across its chest and a black mask around its eyes.
2. Habitat: These birds are native to Southeast Asia, where they primarily inhabit tropical rainforests and montane forests, often preferring dense vegetation and areas with ample insect activity.
3. Feeding Behavior: Banded Broadbills feed primarily on insects and small vertebrates, which they capture by hawking from perches or making short flights to catch prey in mid-air.
4. Breeding Habits: They construct cup-shaped nests made of vegetation and spider silk, which are suspended from the branches of trees, where females lay their eggs and raise their young.
5. Vocalizations: Banded Broadbills produce various vocalizations, including melodious calls and trills, which they use for communication within their social groups and to establish territories.
