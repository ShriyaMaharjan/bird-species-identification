The Eastern Rosella (Platycercus eximius) is native to southeastern Australia, including regions such as Victoria, New South Wales, and Queensland. These colorful parrots are often found in a variety of habitats, ranging from open woodlands to urban areas.

As for its characteristics:
1. **Vibrant Plumage:** Eastern Rosellas are known for their striking and vibrant plumage, featuring a combination of bright red, yellow, green, and blue colors. Their distinctive appearance makes them a visually appealing species.
2. **Facial Markings:** These parrots have distinct facial markings, including white cheek patches and blue patches around the eyes. These markings contribute to their unique and recognizable facial features.
3. **Varied Diet:** Eastern Rosellas have an omnivorous diet that includes seeds, fruits, flowers, and insects. Their versatile feeding habits allow them to adapt to a range of environments and food sources.
4. **Social Behavior:** Eastern Rosellas are often observed in small flocks, displaying social behaviors such as foraging together, engaging in communal roosting, and vocalizing to communicate within their group.
5. **Nesting Habits:** They typically nest in tree hollows, where the female lays a clutch of eggs. Both parents share responsibilities in incubating the eggs and caring for the chicks. Their nesting sites are essential for protecting the vulnerable chicks from predators and environmental factors.
